# CS 330 Portfolio Submission

## Reflection

### How do I approach designing software?  
I approach software design by first breaking down the problem into smaller, manageable pieces. I focus on understanding the requirements and constraints, then translate those into a structure that can be implemented in code. I keep scalability and maintainability in mind so the design can adapt as the project grows.

### What new design skills have your work on the project helped you to craft?  
This project enhanced my ability to design 3D objects by utilizing shapes, textures, lighting, and transformations. I learned how to balance performance with quality, and how to organize my code to separate responsibilities like rendering, camera controls, and shader management.

### What design process did you follow for your project work?  
I followed an iterative process: I began with a simple foundation (basic shapes and transformations) and built up complexity step by step (adding textures, lighting, and interaction). Each milestone allowed me to refine the design before moving forward.

### How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?  
The tactic of starting small and building incrementally can apply to any software project. It helps catch problems early and ensures that each step of development is stable before moving on. This reduces debugging time and makes the code easier to maintain.

### How do I approach developing programs?  
I approach development by coding in small increments, testing frequently, and debugging as I go. I try to focus on one feature at a time, which helps me stay organized and avoid overwhelming complexity.

### What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?  
I used OpenGL-specific strategies like implementing shaders, applying transformations with GLM, and organizing reusable functions for rendering. I also learned to experiment with lighting models and textures to achieve more realistic results.

### How did iteration factor into your development?  
Iteration was central to my success. I constantly tested each new feature (like rotation, scaling, or lighting) before moving on. This iterative cycle made it easier to refine and polish my scene.

### How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones, which led you to the project’s completion?  
Early milestones taught me to focus on correctness first. As the project progressed, I became more confident in experimenting with enhancements. By the end, I had developed a habit of organizing my code more efficiently and thinking ahead about how changes might affect the entire project.
