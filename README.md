CS 330 – Computational Graphics Portfolio Reflection
How do I approach designing software?

When designing software, I begin by understanding the goal of the project and breaking the problem into smaller components. For this 3D scene project, I analyzed the reference image and separated it into individual objects such as the cup, notebooks, pencils, and other desk items. This allowed me to plan which geometric primitives would best represent each object and how they would be positioned in the scene. I learned that good design begins with visualization, planning, and structuring before writing code.

What new design skills has this project helped me craft?

This project helped me develop spatial reasoning and object composition skills. I learned how to design complex objects by combining simple geometric shapes and how to think about scale, proportion, and placement in a 3D world. I also improved my ability to design lighting and camera perspectives to create a more realistic visual experience.

What design process did you follow for your project work?

My process involved analyzing the reference image, identifying the objects, selecting primitive shapes, then gradually constructing the scene piece by piece. I focused on building one object at a time, testing its position, scale, and color before moving on to the next object. Once the scene was assembled, I refined lighting, camera positioning, and textures to improve realism.

How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

Breaking large problems into smaller components will help me in future programming projects, especially in software engineering and game development. The practice of planning objects, relationships, and structure before coding is useful for designing databases, systems, and applications.

How do I approach developing programs?

I approach development iteratively by writing small sections of code, testing frequently, and refining as I go. This prevents large errors and makes debugging easier. During this project, I often tested the scene after adding each object or transformation to ensure everything behaved as expected.

What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

I used modular coding practices by separating scene management, shader logic, and camera controls into different parts of the program. I also practiced debugging visual output rather than just console output, which was a new challenge. Adjusting transformations, lighting, and camera values required experimentation and observation.

How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration was a major part of my process. I frequently adjusted object positions, lighting intensity, and scale to better match the reference image. Each revision improved the realism of the scene. This taught me that development is rarely perfect on the first attempt and that refinement is essential.

How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones?

At the beginning of the course, I focused mainly on making code function correctly. As I progressed, I began to focus more on structure, readability, and efficiency. I learned to think more like a developer by planning ahead, testing frequently, and organizing my code for maintainability.

How can computer science help me reach my goals?

Computer science provides me with the technical skills and problem-solving ability needed for careers in software development, cybersecurity, and database systems. Learning programming and visualization strengthens my analytical thinking and prepares me for complex real-world systems.

How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge for your educational pathway?

This course strengthened my understanding of mathematical transformations, coordinate systems, and rendering pipelines. These concepts are valuable for future coursework in graphics, simulations, and advanced programming topics.

How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge for your professional pathway?

In a professional setting, these skills can be applied to game development, simulation software, data visualization, UI design, and training environments. Understanding how to render and manipulate 3D environments improves my ability to create interactive software and visually communicate information.
